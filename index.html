Michael Jackson

King of pop

His Musical Hits

  • Man In The Mirror - After Jackson's death, Man in the Mirror became the No. 1 single on Itunes downloads in the United States and the United Kingdom. It is one of Jackson's most critically acclaimed songs and was nominated for Record of the Year at the Grammy Awards. It also topped the Billboard Hot 100 for two weeks.

  • Thriller - There is no doubt that this song could not be missing in this list. Thriller changed everything, not only for Jackson himself, but also for the music industry in general, the importance of this piece did not end there. The video opened the doors of the MTV network to black artists, as never before had any artist of color starred in any recording in this medium.

  • Billie Jean - It is one of his best-known songs, and the one that popularized the famous moonwalk, the dance step that became his trademark. Along with the album Thriller, this song made Jackson the first artist to have a pop single, pop album, R&B single and R&B album at number one simultaneously.

  • Don’t stop ‘til you get enough - This song was one of the most important in Jackson's career, it allowed him to win the first Grammy Award, with which he was recognized for 'Best Male R&B Vocal Performance'.

  • Black or white - It is considered the best-selling rock song of the 90's, with more than 4 million copies. Thanks to this, it surpassed Billie Jean's sales and managed to stay for 7 weeks in the first position of the Billboard Hot 100, becoming also number one in the charts of more than 20 countries. The main objective of the song is to promote racial unity.

  • Beat It - This led Jackson to win two Grammy Awards in the categories of Record of the Year and Best Male Rock Vocal Performance. He was inspired to create it during a visit he made to a daycare center in Trenton-New Jersey. Rolling Stone magazine ranked it 337th on its list of the 500 greatest songs of all time.

  • Smooth Criminal - This single is one of Jackson's signature songs, and has appeared on numerous greatest hits albums, including Number Ones, The Essential Michael Jackson, The Ultimate Collection, King of Pop, This Is It, and Immortal. In addition, it served as the theme song for Jackson's 88 film Moonwalker.
